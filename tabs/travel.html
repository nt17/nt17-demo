Travelling to Belo Horizonte


Air Transport
The city of Belo Horizonte is served by two airports: Confins International Airport (CNF), with domestic and international flights, and Pampulha (PLU) with domestic flights only.

Confins International Airport (CNF)
CNF is situated in the Belo Horizonte metropolitan area, in the municipality of Confins. The time from the airport to the Conference location (Pampulha) is about 40 minutes by car. The exact time depends on the traffic conditions.
Address: Rodovia LMG 800, KM 7.9 Confins – MG, 33500-900
More information: International Airport
The transfer from the CNF airport to the Conference location (Pampulha) can be made using taxi or taking a bus to the Pampulha (PLU) airport.

By taxi: You will pay around 110 Brazilian Reais.
By Bus: Outside the airport, look for "Conexão Aeroporto" and, at the ticket counter (see image beside), buy your ticket to the Pampulha local Airport (PLU). Bus price and time-table can be found here (select "Belo Horizonte Bus Station").
Please remember to jump out at PLU! Once you arrive at PLU (about 40 min, but highly dependent on traffic), you can take a taxi to your hotel or to the conference site. You will pay something around 15-25 Brazilian Reais in the taxi. Alternatively you can walk – check distances on google maps (click here for PLU at google maps).

Pampulha Airport (PLU)
PLU is located near the Universidade Federal de Minas Gerais (UFMG) - about 5 minutes by car.
Address: Praça Bagatelle, 204 – Bairro São Luiz -Belo Horizonte – MG. CEP: 31270-705
More Information: Pampulha Airport

Visa
Brazil maintains a fully reciprocal visa policy. Please check what applies for your citizenship at the Immigration division office:
http://www.portalconsular.itamaraty.gov.br/vistos
The registration system (Conftool) provides the option to generate an invitation letter to apply for a VISA. The NT17 organizers advise you to apply for your visa as early as possible, at least 3 months prior to the conference.

Wheather
Belo Horizonte is a 850m hight city, with temperatures ranging from 13 - 25 degrees Celcius in June. June is the coolest and driest season of the year. There is no earthquake, hurricane or other extreme natural phenomena.

Security
Here is our testimony: Yes, there are security issues in Brazil, but when comparing large and touristic cities in Brazil, Belo Horizonte is among the safest. Children don't usually go alone on the streets, mostly because our feeling is that traffic is not mild enough. However, people go out at night and have fun, like everywhere. We never had ourselves and do not know any Brazilian who had stuff robbed from their hotel rooms in Brazil. Still, anyway, you should be careful when crossing streets, carrying large cameras, talking to strangers, etc.

Currency Exchange
Only Real (R$ or BRL) is acceptable as currency at stores and restaurants. You can exchange money at the airport or at the bank. Automated Teller Machines (ATMs) can be found at the airport and throughout the city. (The plural for Real is Reais)

Insurance
Participants are advised to take care of their own personal insurance. NT17 organizers will carry no responsibility in case of an accident, sickness, theft or other property damage.

Time Zone Information
The Belo Horizonte time zone is UTC -3:00.

Electrical Appliances
The type N socket and plug are the official standard in Brazil. Belo Horizonte uses 127 V electricity.
