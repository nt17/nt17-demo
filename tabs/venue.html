Founded in 1927, the Universidade Federal de Minas Gerais/UFMG (Federal University of Minas Gerais) is historically among the five best universities in Brazil, being the largest federal university. With about 49,000 students, the UFMG offers 75 undergraduate degrees, 57 PhD programs, 66 MSc programs, 79 Post-Baccalaureate programs and 38 medical internship programs. UFMG is situated in the Pampulha Campus, which comprises most of its activities; besides, there is the Health Campus, with the Medical Faculty, the Nursing School and the University hospital buildings; the city center sites, including the Faculty of Law and the School of Architecture; the Institute of Agricultural Sciences and the Veterinary farms outside the city. UFMG offers a wide range of museums and places of scientific and cultural interest, such as the Cultural Centre, the UFMG Conservatory, the Natural History Museum, the Botanical Gardens and the Museum of Morphological Science, all of which are open to the public. In addition, it organizes major arts events, such as the Winter and Summer Festivals.The three organizers belong to the Physics Department at UFMG. The Department has 74 Professors and around 200 graduate students, fully engaged in science, with top level ranking from the Brazilian Funding Agency for Graduate Program CAPES. The Department is considerably focused in materials science, with a large number of professors working with nanostructures. The UFMG also counts with Chemistry, Biology and Engineering courses, including hundreds of professors, and a considerable number working in the field of carbon nanotube, graphene and other low dimensional materials. The UFMG professors lead the two most important Brazilian projects in the carbon nanotube field, which are the National Institute of Science and Technology in Carbon Nanostructures, and the Technology Center for Carbon Nanotubes. The first puts together, annually, hundreds of researchers from all over Brazil to discuss science and to have some fun together.

Space and Conference Facilities


With 640 sits, the Auditório Nobre (Noble auditorium) will be used for the main event. It is located in the CAD1 (Centro de Atividades Didáticas 1), in the core of the University campus, sharing the students atmosphere and infrastructure. The poster sessions will take place at the Great Festival Room, in the same building, a place that is commonly used for graduation ceremonies and other festive events.
