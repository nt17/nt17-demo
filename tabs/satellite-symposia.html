  • Chief Organizer: Yoshiyuki Miyamoto - AIST, Japan
  • Local Organizer: Ricardo Wagner Nunes - UFMG, Brazil

Scope
The CCTN satellite meetings, since 2006 Nagano, had focused on computational/theoretical challenges to understand/predict properties and formation processes of carbon nanotubes (CNTs) and related materials. Suggestion of the various chiral structures in CNTs by Sumio Iijima in 1991 made theorists aware of an importance of material design by computational-science approaches (and by inspiration) prior to experimental identification. Thanks to the growing power of computational resources as well as development of computational algorisms, designing material-formation has also become our accessible target. In the CCTN17 satellite meeting, various theoretical approaches will be presented and we will discuss future computational approaches (maybe) based on information science for emerging materials.
